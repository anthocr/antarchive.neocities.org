# Abundance
#ai #politics #alternative-energy #reading 

Author: Ezra Klein & Derek Thompson
### Rating
4/5
## Synopsis
Abundance is framed as the outline of a solution to many *supply issues* plaguing us in the modern day. The book is framed with a liberal mindset, since that is the mindset most adaptable to what this book is proposing. It outlines the past failures and successes from both sides of the political spectrum, with much focus being on **public works projects** and other **advancements** that humanity has made.

The book highlights the systematic flaws of the construction and development ecosystem in the U.S. and specifically California. The issue with many modern developments is that there is a heavy amount of red tape that is involved with building **anything**. This is especially true in California, a state with the most rules and regulations in the U.S. The failure of the *high speed rail project* is a great example of this, the projects mismanagement as well as overwhelming bureaucracy has made it a quite catastrophic failure.

The lack of alternative energy is also a main concern in modern times with technologies like artificial intelligence requiring more and more energy and other resources to operate. One of the major arguments in this book is that **we have not effectively utilized our inventions**, instead leaving other countries to do the application for us. It's explained that despite being the prime inception of *solar technology*, we have not effectively deployed useful solar technology in the U.S. We shut down more alternative energy sources per year than we create, and this is a huge problem in **sustainability**. 
## Thoughts
If you are interested in *alternative energy*, *modern politics*, or **a way to create a sustainable future in the U.S.** I would highly recommend this book. While the book is framed in a liberal mindset, however it analyzes the systematic faults that have plagued the country for decades. This book provides insight into how the problems we have were created, and uses the past to build a framework for how we could begin to develop a solution.
