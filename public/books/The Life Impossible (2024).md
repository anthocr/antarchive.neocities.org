# The Life Impossible
#mathematics #philosophy #mystery #novel #reading

Author: Matt Haig
### Rating
4.2/5
## Thoughts
This book's premise is relatively simple with minimal exposition. The main character, Grace Witherspoon is a former math teacher that has lost her husband and son, who are very important to her journey. The book is framed as a letter to a former student of hers who has reached out to her as she is struggling in life. Grace sends her a manifesto documenting her journey through Ibiza, with her being sent there by a long lost former friend.

The book slowly introduces supernatural elements which become more prevalent as the story goes forward. I thought their introduction was well done and conflicts well with the Grace, with her initially wanting to reject any supernatural elements. The book also excels in providing detailed and almost vivid depictions of her 'powers' that she gains. The book excels so much at it that the descriptions can get tiresome at times, floundering over itself a bit. However, it made it much easier to 'see' the fictional Ibiza (*the island is not fictional, just the book*) that Grace is traveling through.
## Themes
There are many impactful themes that this book tries to tackle through Grace's character. She struggles with her age, trauma, her newfound *greatness*, and also her relationships with the other characters. One of the core themes that resonated with me the most is **finding beauty in life**. Throughout the book Grace allows herself to enjoy life more as the story progresses, initially held back by the loss of her son and the guilt that comes along with it. But by the end of the book, Grace has finally allowed herself to remember her son for the person that he is, and not the guilt and trauma that followed his death.

She has learned to enjoy life and the **contrast** between the good and the bad. This resonated with me deeply, since I've had a similar experience. Our minds want to remember experiences as good or bad, black and white, but that is only our perception of the **memory**. Good experiences are corrupt by the hurt that followed. By overcoming her trauma and embracing the beauty of her own life and others she was able to heal and truly **live**.
## Conclusion
This book has a great narrative, even being dragged out at times. I enjoyed the **mystery**, **introspection**, and **character** that this book had. I would've enjoyed a little more that was left up to interpretation with most of the mystery being solved for the most part by the end, although this isn't purely a mystery book. If you are looking for a story to potentially help you in a dark place, I would recommend this one.
