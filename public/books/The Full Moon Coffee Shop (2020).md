# The Full Moon Coffee Shop (2020)
#reading #astrology #novel 

Author: Mai Mochizuki
### Rating
3/5
## Synopsis
The Full Moon Coffee Shop is a popup coffee shop that arrives on a full moon when people need guidance. The staff of this shop are composed of various animals including cats and tortoises. The staff serves drinks that are meant to alleviate a patron's problems or lead them along a better path.

The book consists of three main parts focusing on three different characters; a screenwriter, a romantically stuck movie director, a hopeful hairstylist and a technologically challenged website designer, with their stories overlapping occasionally. 
## Thoughts
This book depicts the character's lives through **astrology**, a main focus in this book. I personally don't know much about the subject but the fundamentals are explained. The conclusions of the character's arcs feel slightly arbitrary since all the aspects of their lives are attributed to different astrological phases.

I also feel that the way this book is written feels very flat. This book is translated from Japanese, which likely can be attributed to some of the plain language. However, I believe the major flaw of this story is that everything feels very nonconsequential.

I wouldn't recommend this book to most unless you really enjoy astrology and animals.
