# Democracy Awakening: Notes on the State of America (2023)
#politics #reading #democracy

Author: Heather Cox Richardson
### Rating
5/5
## Synopsis
If you have wondered recently, "*how did we get here?*" then this book might provide some guidance. Richardson's narrative dives into the history of democracy in the Americas from the inception to modern times. This book was written in the midst of Donald Trump's impeachment case in 2019 and while not focusing entirely on Trump, it relates many of its ideas towards his obsession with authoritarian power.

## Thoughts
Many recent events have led many Americans to feel hopeless about the future of the country, including myself. I'm sure many could agree that we are experiencing the worst presidency in the country's history at this moment. However, this book has give me some hope that the forces of good can still prevail in these troubling times. That is because we have experienced the same problems in previous generations. 

Democracy is a continued experiment. The founding fathers (**being flawed individuals**) argued that the citizens of this country are **smart**, and **capable** enough to govern themselves. It can be argued that they meant strictly white cis wealthy citizens, but this was later rewritten to define **all Americans** through the 14th amendment. However, the current administration does not believe this inherit truth.

The 45th (now 47th) president of the Unites States believes its citizens are **not capable** of governing themselves. He as well as his followers (*not necessarily all Republicans*) believe that a few wealthy autocrats should instead govern the lower and middle classes. Unfortunately, wealthy people already hold a vast amount of power in capitalism. Many of Trump's actions can be attributed to **undermining**, **eroding**, or **directly attacking** our democracy. Notable events include January 6th, the undermining of the election process, general disregard for separation of powers, and the list goes on.

If you want to know more about how exactly we got to this point in our country as well as the history of how it originated I highly recommend this book.
